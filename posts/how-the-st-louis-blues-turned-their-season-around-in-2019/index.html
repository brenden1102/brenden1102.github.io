The Improbable Turnaround of the 2019 St. Louis Blues

Author

Brenden Evers

Published

November 30, 2023

On Jan. 3, 2019 The St Louis Blues found themselves in last place in the NHL and were consistently losing games by a wide margin. Fans were beginning to check out. Tickets for late season home games at the time could be found for 15 dollars or less on the resale market. However, the team ended up pulling off one of the most incredible turnarounds in sports history. So, what happened? On Nov 9, The Blues fired their head coach, Mike Yeo, after a 2-0 loss to the LA Kings who at the time were the only team with fewer points than them. The team promoted assistant coach Craig Berube to interim head coach. By early January the Blues had fallen to last place in the league having played almost 50% of their schedule. This is when St. Louis began to climb out of the league’s basement. Lucky enough for the Blues there many of the teams ahead of them in the Western Conference were struggling. By the all-star break they were only 3 points out of a wild card spot. From Jan. 20, through Feb. 11, St. Louis won 11 games in a row.

NhlLogos <- read_csv("nhl_logos.csv") 
HockeyLogs19 <- read_csv("hockeylogs2019.csv") |> 
  mutate(month = floor_date(date, "month")) 
GoalieStats19 <- read_csv("NHL 2018-19 Goalie Stats.csv") |> 
  filter(GS >= 30) |> 
  filter(SO >0)
GoalieShutouts <- GoalieStats19 |> 
  filter(SO >= 4)
GoodGAA <- GoalieStats19 |> 
  filter(GAA <= 2.48)
JordanB <- GoalieStats19 |> 
  filter(Player == "Jordan Binnington") 
HhlShots <- HockeyLogs19 |> 
  group_by(team_name) |> 
  filter(team_name!="St. Louis Blues") |> 
  summarise(Shots_For = sum(ff/82),
            Shots_against = sum(fa/82),
            Winz = sum(x_2 == "W")) |> 
  left_join(NhlLogos, by=c("team_name"="espn_team_name"))
BluezShotztoJan <-  HockeyLogs19 |> 
  group_by(team_name) |> 
  filter(team_name == "St. Louis Blues", date < as.Date("2019-01-03")) |> 
  summarise(Games = n(), 
            Winz = sum(x_2 == "W"), 
            ff = sum(ff), 
            fa = sum(fa)) |> 
  mutate(Shots_For = ff/Games,
            Shots_against = fa/Games) |> 
  left_join(NhlLogos, by=c("team_name"="espn_team_name"))
BluezShotzaftJan <-  HockeyLogs19 |> 
  group_by(team_name) |> 
  filter(team_name == "St. Louis Blues", date >= as.Date("2019-01-03")) |> 
  summarise(Games = n(), 
            Winz = sum(x_2 == "W"), 
            ff = sum(ff), 
            fa = sum(fa)) |> 
  mutate(Shots_For = ff/Games,
            Shots_against = fa/Games) |> 
  left_join(NhlLogos, by=c("team_name"="espn_team_name"))

PlayoffTeams <- c("Tampa Bay Lightning", "Boston Bruins", "Dallas Stars", "Columbus Blue Jackets", "St. Louis Blues", "Colorado Avalanche", "Nashville Predators", "Calgary Flames", "Carolina Hurricanes", "San Jose Sharks", "New York Islanders", "Washington Capitals", "Toronto Maple Leafs", "Pittsburgh Penguins", "Winnipeg Jets", "Vegas Golden Knights")

MonthlyRecord <- HockeyLogs19 |> 
  group_by(team_name, month) |> 
  summarize(
    Wins = sum(x_2 == "W"),
    Losses = sum(x_2 == "L")
  ) |> 
  mutate(
    CumulativeWins = cumsum(Wins),
    CumulativeLosses = cumsum(Losses)    
  ) |> 
  filter(team_name %in% PlayoffTeams) 

Stl <- MonthlyRecord |>
  filter(team_name == "St. Louis Blues")
Tbl <- MonthlyRecord |> 
  filter(team_name == "Tampa Bay Lightning")

ggplot() +

Much of the credit for the Blues climb back to relevance can be attributed to the success of rookie goaltender Jordan Binnington. In just over 30 regular season starts Binnington posted strong numbers including five shutouts. Binnington also finished the season leading atop the goals against leader board.

The Blues also stepped it up on both defense and offense. From Jan. 3 to the end of the season St. Louis averaged 6 more shots or shot attempts per game and averaged one less shot against per game. With improved goaltending and more chances to score the Blues went on an incredible run just to make it to the postseason where they won their first championship in franchise history.
